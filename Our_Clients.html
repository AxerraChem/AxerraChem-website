The Operators We Serve

Supporting Clients
Globally Across
Diverse Assets and Geologies.

Axerra works with oil and gas operators across a broad range of geographic regions and asset types. While our engagements are global in scope, our primary operational focus is on projects located in the Middle East, South America, Central America, and Europe, where reservoirs often present elevated technical and operational complexity.

Our clients include both large integrated operators and smaller, technically focused producers. Engagements range from single‑asset evaluations to multi‑field programs, with chemistry applied as part of ongoing production, stimulation, or integrity management strategies. The scale of the operator is less important than the nature of the challenge being addressed.

Asset Complexity

Complex Reservoir and Production Environments

A significant portion of Axerra's work involves assets where recovery is limited by geological complexity, fluid behavior, or operating conditions, rather than by hydrocarbons in place.

layers

Low-Permeability or Heterogeneous Formations

Reservoirs with uneven flow distribution, channelling, or low matrix permeability where standard waterflood or production programs underperform.

water_drop

Heavy-Oil and High-Viscosity Systems

Crudes with viscosities exceeding 100 cSt where thermal methods are uneconomic or impractical, and chemical viscosity reduction delivers the required mobility improvement.

science

Asphaltene, Wax, or Water-Handling Challenges

Reservoirs with elevated asphaltene deposition, paraffin crystallization, or complex produced water management requirements that standard field programs cannot address.

thermostat

Wide Thermal, Pressure, or Salinity Ranges

Fields operating across extreme temperature, pressure, or brine salinity conditions where chemistry must be formulated specifically for the operating envelope rather than applied off-the-shelf.

Axerra's engagement model and product portfolio are structured to operate in these conditions, where standard chemical programs often require adaptation or redevelopment to remain effective.
