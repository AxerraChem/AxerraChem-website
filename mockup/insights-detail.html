Nanoparticle-Surfactant Synergy in Chemical EOR: Silica Stabilization of Foam in High-Salinity Carbonate Reservoirs

Investigation into the stabilizing effects of silica nanoparticles on foam stability within high-salinity carbonate reservoirs. Results show a 3× improvement in foam half-life and 18% incremental recovery uplift over surfactant-only injection.

Authors

Dr. S. Al-Rashidi, M. Chen, P. Vasquez — Axerra R&D

Journal

Axerra Technical Series, Vol. 4

Key Results

  • 3× Foam half-life improvement with optimized NP-surfactant ratio
  • +18% Incremental oil recovery vs. surfactant-only injection
  • 200k ppm TDS — validated performance ceiling in high-salinity brine

1. Background

Foam-assisted EOR has long been recognized as a powerful mobility control mechanism in gas-flooding and CO₂ EOR programs. However, foam instability under reservoir conditions — particularly in high-salinity, high-temperature carbonate systems — has historically limited its commercial application. Surfactant adsorption onto carbonate surfaces reduces active concentrations below the critical micelle concentration, collapsing foam lamellae within hours of injection.

2. Hypothesis

Axerra's research team hypothesized that surface-modified silica nanoparticles (NPs), when co-injected with an anionic surfactant blend, would preferentially adsorb at the gas-liquid interface of foam lamellae, providing steric stabilization independent of surfactant concentration. This mechanism, known as Pickering stabilization, had been demonstrated in laboratory-scale studies but not validated in reservoir-analog conditions above 100°C or 100,000 ppm TDS.

3. Conclusions & Field Implications

The results confirm that silica NP co-injection at an optimized loading of 0.1 wt% provides a statistically significant 3× improvement in foam half-life under reservoir-analog conditions. The corresponding 18% incremental recovery uplift, measured over 2.0 pore volumes injected, represents a substantial improvement over conventional surfactant foam programs, particularly in high-salinity carbonate systems where surfactant adsorption has historically undermined performance.

Field deployment will require careful NP surface modification to prevent aggregation during transport, and injection system compatibility assessments for existing wellbore infrastructure. Axerra is currently evaluating two candidate fields for a Phase 1 pilot program targeting Q3 2026.
