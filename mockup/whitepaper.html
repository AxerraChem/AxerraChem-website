Abstract

Asphaltene and paraffin co-deposition represents one of the most costly flow assurance challenges in heavy oil production. Conventional approaches — mechanical pigging, thermal treatment, and solvent remediation — are reactive by nature, incurring operational disruption and substantial OPEX. This paper introduces Axerra's Molecular Deposition Prevention (MDP) framework, a proactive chemical strategy that targets the nucleation stage of both asphaltene flocculation and wax crystallization. Laboratory validation across eight distinct crude oil systems demonstrates that MDP treatments achieve up to 94% deposition reduction, with a corresponding IFT reduction below 10⁻³ mN/m under high-pressure, high-temperature conditions.

1. Introduction

Overview of asphaltene-wax deposition mechanisms, economic impact in mature heavy oil fields, and the limitations of conventional remediation strategies.

2. Chemistry Framework

Molecular architecture of Axerra's co-crystal inhibitors. Discussion of surfactant-polymer synergies and active group positioning for wax nuclei disruption.

3. Laboratory Results

Cold finger deposition tests, cold plate microscopy, and core flooding results across 8 crude systems ranging from 12° to 28° API gravity.

4. Field Application

Injection design methodology, dosing protocols, and monitoring framework for continuous downhole treatment programs.

5. Economics

Comparative cost analysis versus pigging, hot oiling, and solvent squeeze programs. Break-even analysis across three production rate scenarios.

6. Conclusions

Summary of MDP framework performance, applicability criteria, and roadmap for full-field commercial deployment in heavy oil assets.
